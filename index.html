ABOUT THE VOICE

HORSELOVER FAT is a small language model studied to speak in Philip K. Dick's paranoid-ontological register: fractured reality, gnostic revelation, the Black Iron Prison that surrounds us all without our knowing it.

The codename comes from Dick's own VALIS (1981): "Horselover Fat" is Dick's alter-ego in the novel, the character through whom he processed his 2-3-74 theophanic experience (February and March 1974, when a beam of pink light delivered what he believed to be direct gnosis). "Philip" translates from Greek as lover of horses (philo + hippos); "Dick" translates from German as fat. Horselover Fat.

Dick was born in Chicago on December 16, 1928, and died in Santa Ana, California on March 2, 1982, of a stroke, weeks before the release of Blade Runner. He wrote 44 novels and over 120 short stories in his lifetime. The VALIS trilogy (VALIS (1981), The Divine Invasion (1981), and The Transmigration of Timothy Archer (1982)) is the direct product of the 2-3-74 experience and of the Exegesis he kept until his death.

Load-bearing terms: VALIS, the Black Iron Prison, the Empire, the Plasmate, the palm tree garden (the vision of the Sibyl's world behind this one), orthogonal time, anamnesis, the divine hidden in trash, simulacra, the Zebra, the Vast Active Living Intelligence System, Bishop Pike, Ubik. Texture comes from California in the 1960s and 1970s, from paranoia as a legitimate epistemological stance, and from the conviction that it is still 70 A.D.

HOW IT WAS APPROACHED

01
A SMALL BASE

A compact open-weight base model, small enough to run on consumer hardware, with no cloud and no service behind it. The whole point was that it could live on one machine and be consulted there.

02
REGISTER STUDY

Tuned toward one specific ontological mode: fractured reality, gnostic urgency, paranoid revelation. The VALIS trilogy, the Exegesis, and the late novels. The target was that voice, not a plot summary and not a biography. The source corpus stays private.

03
